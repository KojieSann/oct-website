Foundation Week

Olivarez College Tagaytay's 20th Founding Anniversary celebration comes to an end.

Patrisha Maranan

Mikhaela Bergado, Ma Reinna Mandap, Chrysler Versoza, Eugene Pascual

Decades Of Excellence

Olivarez College Tagaytay (OCT) recently celebrated its 20th Founding Anniversary with week-long activities and events that concluded with an awarding and closing program held at the OCT Gymnasium on March 16, 2024. The foundation week was a reflection of the school's past achievements and growth, while also looking forward to the future with hope and youth.

During the closing program, winners of different competitions were announced, including E-Games, Sportsfest, Cheerdance, and Overall Champions for the founding anniversary.

  • CHAMPION - STEM Strand
  • 1ST RUNNER UP - HUMSS Strand
  • 2ND RUNNER UP - BSN & BSCrim Department

For two decades, OCT has been a beacon of knowledge and light that has guided generations of students. In the coming years, the school will continue to educate the Mind, Body, and Soul with a H.E.A.R.T.

General Student Orientation

Olivarez College Tagaytay successfully welcomed college freshmen, transferees, and returnees from every department into the academic year 2024-2025 at the Tagaytay Country Hotel (TCH) Pavilion on the 21st day of August 2024.

Lyza Morillo

Rasel Vidallon, Brix Mendoza

OCT Pushed Through Academic Year 2024-2025

Dr. Jean Rizza Dela Cruz, College Dean, opened the day by assuring the students of a quality education and improvements for the Olivarians. “What we can assure you is that this is a new opportunity for learning and growth," said Dr. Dela Cruz in her opening remarks.

“Rest assured that our faculty members and our members of the administration will be supporting you all the way, so we will be able to achieve your dreams and your goals in life," she added as she talked about the challenges that will be encountered along the student’s academic journey.

Moreover, a surprise was revealed in the orientation, as OCT’s classroom, laboratories, and other facilities are now fully equipped with air conditioning units.

In addition to welcoming the students, OCT held a raffle during the program to distribute merchandise such as tote bags and mugs, as well as cash prizes and a brand new laptop.

Discussion of policies, reminders, and guidance was also done by the respective heads of each department.

The said orientation was conducted in two batches: students from the Bachelor of Science in Criminology, Bachelor of Science in Accountancy and Accounting Information System, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology held their orientation in the morning.

On the other hand, the second batch held theirs in the afternoon and consisted of students from the Bachelor of Science in Nursing, Bachelor of Science in Tourism Management, Bachelor of Science in Hospitality Management, Bachelor of Elementary Education, as well as the students of Caregiving and Bread and Pastry Production (TESDA short courses).

Lastly, BS Information Technology was recognized and topped as the outstanding enrollment growth rate for the academic year, while BS Criminology remains the most populated program in OCT.

ISA Orientation

On August 15, 2014, Olivarez College Tagaytay and Parañaque hosted an Institutional Sustainability Assessment (ISA) orientation for the entire institution

Ann Rachel Y. Cuadra, LPT, MAEd

Lito Bajado Jr., MSHRM

Olivarez College: Moving Towards Greater Learning Competence!

At this historic event, educators and support staff from two locations came together for a full day of stimulating workshops aimed at improving curriculum and advancing the campuses' sustainability initiatives. Participants in the orientation sought to improve institutional norms, processes, and curriculum for students and other stakeholders. In addition to providing for the demands of the present needs, Olivarez College is committed to establishing new benchmarks for sustainability in education. This proactive strategy demonstrates a dedication to providing excellence and ongoing development.

Graduation Ceremony

On June 7, 2024, the 18th Commencement Exercises of OCT marked a significant day for the graduation world for the class of 2024.

Ann Rachel Y. Cuadra, LPT, MAEd

Peter Jr. C. Sesma, LPT

Bravo Batch 2024! The 18th Commencement Exercises of OCT was a great success!

The event focused on "Demystifying Excellence in the Better Normal Landscape," highlighting the students' readiness to move on to the next phase of their lives.

The awards ceremony, which took place at the Convention Center, was attended by excited families, esteemed faculty members, administrators, and honored guests. The commencement theme, "Demystifying Excellence in the Better Normal Landscape," signified a shift from traditional graduation ceremonies while remaining an enjoyable and significant experience.

Dr. Rommel Pilapil Sergio, a scholar and lecturer, delivered a moving speech emphasizing the need to embrace change for better achievement. His words profoundly impacted both the graduates and the audience, stressing the importance of intelligent visioning and adapting to the new normal.

Special awards were given to outstanding students excelling in academic achievement, leadership, and community service

As tassels were turned and caps were thrown, the Class of 2024 officially graduated, feeling palpable excitement and a sense of achievement. They celebrated their accomplishments and looked forward to the future.

Equipped with knowledge and fond memories from their years at OCT, the graduates move forward confidently, ready to face the following stages of their lives. They are inspired to contribute to creating a better normal after the pandemic, building on the knowledge and inspiration gained from their graduation.
