THE SPECTRUM is the official research journal of the Senior High School Program. Within the pages of THE SPECTRUM, a rich tapestry of research outputs awaits discovery, spanning disciplines and perspectives. Encompassing the domains of Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM), Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM), Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS), General Academic Strand (GAS), and Technical-Vocational-Livelihood Track (TVL), this repository encapsulates the ingenuity and diligence of students. The launch of this yearly electronic research magazine is a significant turning point in the academic careers of those who contribute, representing the fruition of unceasing devotion and thorough assessment. Each article is a monument to academic rigor and intellectual curiosity, carefully examined by panel members and the editorial board and guided by committed research advisers. THE SPECTRUM promises to educate the school community, the general public, and future researchers when it sets out on its maiden voyage. These research projects inspire a new generation of scholars to investigate and develop, going beyond revealing fresh insights and calling toward unexplored areas of investigation, serving as a guiding light in the rich field of academic inquiry, encouraging a spirit of inquiry and high-caliber research.

Guided by the Vision of Olivarez College Tagaytay (OCT), the Center for Research, Publication, Planning and Development shall become the leading knowledge hub in the province of Cavite that is known for research that embodies excellence in the transfer of relevant and innovative knowledge through holistic and value-laden education and sustainable pro-active extension towards social transformation, exploring initiatives, and possibilities to address local and global possibilities.

Olivarez College Tagaytay (OCT) is committed to translate, advance, transfer, and utilize relevant and innovative knowledge through research engagement.

To attain the Research Vision 2023, OCT aims to:

  • Generate credible and innovative research production that will significantly contribute towards local, national and global development.
  • Increase engagement in classroom-based action research that enhances instruction or teaching-learning processes.
  • Develop quality researchers in residence, who are skillful in the use of various research methodologies, proficient in the use of research tools to analyze data towards formulation of policies and programs designed to improve the quality of life of Filipinos.
  • Collaborate and strengthen local, national and international linkages to increase research engagement and synergy in disseminating and utilizing basic research, applied research and creative works.
  • Establish sustainable processes to cultivate an Olivarian research culture.
